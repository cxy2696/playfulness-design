Playful Antiques

Enhancing Fun and Reflection in UX Design

Xinyi Cui, INFO6940 2025 FA

Problem & Design Concept

  • Reminiscence therapy aids reflection but often feels task-oriented (Woods et al., 2018).
  • My AI web app reimagines memories with random twists (e.g., clockwork adventures), fostering curiosity and non-utilitarian fun.
  • Ties to course: Supports exploration/reflection over defined tasks (Gaver et al., 2004).

What is Play?

  • Ludic designs: Ambiguity, openness, curiosity (non-utilitarian).
  • Play (UX) vs. Game (system): Focus on reflection/exploration over rules/objectives.

Mind-Body Fun

  • Fun as contesting control: Surrender/looseness (e.g., Broncomatic).
  • Immersion: Body as play
  • Applied: Tool suggests physical actions; adapts to emotions like Brainball.

Social Fun & Magic Circle

  • Cooperation/competition with unwritten rules.
  • Magic circle: Enchanted zone for play (Sniderman, 1999).
  • Applied: Branching stories create pervasive playgrounds, blurring game/IRL (in real life).

Antique Inspirations & Affective Motion

  • Medieval robots as precursors (500-1500 CE).
  • Affective/abstract motion: Enhances acceptance (Hoffman & Ju, 2014).
  • Applied: Tool uses clockwork motifs for wonder, like Apple's 2025 prototype (targeted 2027 launch).
