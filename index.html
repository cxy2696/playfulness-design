Playful AI in Reminiscence

This web page showcases a design concept for an AI-powered reminiscence tool that infuses playfulness into the process of recalling memories. Inspired by the "Delightful AI" course (Module 1), it draws on concepts like openness, ambiguity, curiosity, exploration, and non-utilitarian fun to make reminiscence engaging and reflective, rather than purely therapeutic. This could be especially useful for older adults in reminiscence therapy, but with a twist of joy and surprise.

Design Concept

The AI acts as a playful companion that takes user-input memories and transforms them into interactive, story-like narratives with random fun elements. It encourages voluntary involvement by letting users explore "what if" scenarios, adds affection through warm, personalized language, and promotes reflection by prompting deeper thoughts. Unlike traditional reminiscence tools, this design "squares the magic circle" by blending real memories with playful, game-like ambiguitiesâ€”turning everyday recollections into adventures.

Key AI Features (Conceptual):

  • Generates personalized stories from user memories using generative AI (e.g., similar to chatbots like Remi or MemoryLane).
  • Adapts based on emotions (e.g., via facial recognition like in LONG-REMI) to keep sessions positive and engaging.
  • Incorporates gamified elements, such as proverb completions or rhythm-based memory games, to evoke physical and emotional responses.
  • Uses controlled randomness to introduce surprises, fostering curiosity and exploration.

Playful Elements from the Course

Drawing from Module 1:

  • Openness and Ambiguity: Memories are reimagined with unexpected twists, like adding magical elements or alternate endings.
  • Curiosity and Exploration: Users are prompted to "explore" branches in the story, like in pervasive games (e.g., PacManhattan).
  • Affection and Reflection: Warm, empathetic responses encourage emotional connections and self-reflection, similar to affective robotic motion in Pinokio Lamp.
  • Non-Utilitarian Fun: Focuses on ludic activities (curiosity-driven, not task-oriented), blurring the line between play and real life, as in the Drift Table.
  • Mind-Body Push-and-Pull: Incorporates body-as-play elements, like suggesting physical actions (e.g., dancing to a recalled song).
  • Magic Circle: Creates an "enchanted zone" where users can safely lose and regain control, inspired by Brainball or Broncomatic.
